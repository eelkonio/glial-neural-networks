# Emulated Glia Networks Interfacing with Artificial Neural Networks

## Overview

This document set explores how emulated glial cell networks could enhance, direct, and fundamentally reshape artificial neural networks. Unlike neurons, glial cells operate through chemical signaling, physical mobility, and structural remodeling — forming a parallel computational substrate that modulates, prunes, and reorganizes neural circuits.

## Core Thesis

Biological neural networks are not neuron-only systems. Glial cells outnumber neurons in many brain regions and perform computations that operate on fundamentally different timescales, spatial scales, and chemical modalities than neuronal signaling. An emulated glia network layered onto existing ANNs would introduce:

1. **A slow modulatory layer** that adjusts network behavior over longer timescales than gradient descent
2. **Mobile computational agents** that can physically relocate their influence across the network
3. **Chemical multi-channel signaling** that operates orthogonally to weight-based information flow
4. **Active structural remodeling** including connection removal, pathway insulation, and resource allocation
5. **Distributed memory** that is not stored in weights but in the spatial configuration of glial processes

This is not merely adding another neural network layer — it is introducing an entirely different computational paradigm that coexists with and governs the neural network.
